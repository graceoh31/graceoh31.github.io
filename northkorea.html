The Challenges of the North Korean People



North Korea is a real place with real people who face real problems.

A narrative often untold in the media is that of the North Korean people, who are denied basic human rights and are forced to live closed off from the rest of the world with denied access to the outside.

No freedom of movement, no freedom of speech, no freedom of information.

Public executions designed to instill fear amongst the people.

Five political prison camps hold an estimated 80,000 to 120,000 people.
